Frequently Asked Questions:

What is this?

Semantics-preserving word cloud visulization tool. A word cloud consists of the most important words in that document. Each word is printed in a given font and scaled by a factor roughly proportional to its importance. The printed words are arranged without overlap and tightly packed into rectangular shape. Your word clouds can be tweaked with various fonts, layouts, and color schemes. Read the description for more details.

What documents can you visualize?

You may enter any textual document, the URL of a blog or webpage, or a link to a pdf document. The system can also visualize a "comment cloud" of YouTube videos.

Can i visualize Twitter?

Enter "twitter: search_query" (without quotes) to parse and create a word cloud for 100 most recent tweets returned by Twitter Search for a given query. You may customize the results by specifying search operators. The query "twitter: graph drawing size:500 type:popular lang:en include:retweets" builds a word cloud for 500 most popular tweets and retweets written in English containing the words 'graph' and 'drawing'.

Can i save my word cloud as jpeg/gif/png/pdf?

At the moment, a cloud is generated as SVG file, which can be downloaded by using a link at the top-right corner of the cloud. In order to convert it to another format, use a vector graphics editor, like Inkscape.

May i see your source code?

Yes! Source code for the entire system is available on Github.
